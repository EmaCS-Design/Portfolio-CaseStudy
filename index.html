Case Study 01

instant THICK

Late 2024 – Present · Liquid Launch Complete · Powder Range Ongoing

"Built the brand infrastructure that turned a written-off product line into a category best-seller — without a design team."

Context

instant THICK is a medical-grade food thickener for people living with dysphagia — a swallowing condition managed across a complex, multi-channel network spanning hospitals, aged care facilities, in-home care, clinical settings, and everyday consumers. The product exists in two formats: Powder (measured single-serve) and Liquid (single-serve and bulk-serve), both achieving the same clinical thickening outcomes. In a high-trust medical category, brand credibility is not optional.

The Problem

Despite serving a complex, multi-tier audience in one of the most trust-sensitive categories in healthcare, the brand had no identity, no visual system, and no user consideration at any level:

  • Looked like a direct-from-manufacturer product — zero brand credibility in clinical or consumer environments
  • No visual logic to distinguish between formats, serving methods, or use cases across B2B and B2C channels
  • Internal teams could not produce on-brand materials without a designer — creating bottlenecks across all channels
  • The Liquid product line had previously failed in market and been discontinued
Before

instant THICK before — manufacture-label product, no visual system, no brand identity

What I Built

Working directly with the Head of Marketing, I designed and built the entire brand system independently — structured to serve every tier of the channel, from hospital procurement through to personal home use:

  • Established a colour-coded visual language: Blue for single-serve preparation, Orange for bulk-serve — giving clinicians, aged care staff, and everyday users an immediate, intuitive navigation system
  • Built a multi-tier brand system designed to work across hospital, aged care, in-home care, and direct consumer — each channel with appropriate language, hierarchy, and collateral
  • Strategically repositioned and relaunched the Liquid line as a new product — new identity, new packaging logic, new channel collateral — re-entering a market it had previously failed in
  • Created a scalable design system enabling internal stakeholders to execute on-brand independently across B2B and B2C
  • Produced a full suite of clinical and retail collateral — sell sheets, mixing guides, nutritional comparisons, and packaging across all SKUs
After

instant THICK 2026 — complete clinical brand system across Powder and Liquid ranges

Outcome
  • Liquid product line — previously discontinued — relaunched successfully and became the best-selling product in its category
  • 113,000 units sold across 3 Liquid SKUs within 18 months of relaunch — and still growing
  • Internal teams now operate independently within the brand system across all channels
  • Powder range rollout currently underway using the same scalable framework

Case Study 02
Medical Snack · FMCG · Global

Screamies

Brand System Build · Ongoing

"Not just making a medical product approachable — bridging the gap between clinical function, the novelty of the product, and the emotional joy it was always meant to deliver."

Context

Screamies is a no-melt, shelf-stable ice cream originally developed for people living with dysphagia. Servable from room temperature through to frozen — safe, convenient, and accessible any time, anywhere. High in nutritional value and inclusive for all ages, it is a global product operating under food compliance and regulatory requirements. The product sits at a genuinely rare intersection: it functions as a medical-grade nutritional snack, yet it is ice cream — a food category loaded with familiarity, novelty, and emotional resonance.

The Problem

The brand existed at the intersection of three things that were not speaking to each other — and the visual identity was failing all three simultaneously:

  • The functional side — its medical-grade purpose, nutritional credentials, and dysphagia safety — was invisible in how the brand presented itself
  • The novelty — no-melt, shelf-stable, any-time-anywhere ice cream is genuinely surprising — was buried under a disconnected, factory-feel appearance
  • The emotional side — the familiar joy of ice cream, and the inclusiveness designed for everyone including people with special medical conditions — was absent from the brand entirely
  • No brand hierarchy existed to support product line growth or future category expansion
  • As a global product, materials needed to hold up across compliance and regulatory environments — which the existing system could not support
Before

Screamies before — disconnected from function, novelty, and the audience it was designed for

What I Built

A complete brand identity and system built to hold three things at once — without one undermining the others:

  • Brought product function to the forefront — the no-melt, shelf-stable, any-time-anywhere benefit became a brand asset, leading with what the product does and who it is designed for
  • Built a full brand hierarchy — Primary, Secondary, and Secondary-Lite tiers — creating a scalable framework with room for future categories to grow within the same system
  • Kept the identity fun, casual, and emotionally familiar — so the medical purpose empowers rather than distances the audience from the joy of the product
  • Rebuilt the Sorbet category from the ground up — formerly a failed launch, relaunched with an entirely new design system and the addition of a new Lemon flavour, successfully re-entering the market as a new category
  • Designed to be globally compliant and executable across all touchpoints without designer dependency
After

Screamies after — full brand system, Sorbet category rebuild, and scalable visual hierarchy

Outcome
  • Sorbet category successfully rebuilt and relaunched — a previously failed launch re-entering market with new credibility, new design, and a new Lemon flavour
  • Sales continue to grow — brand strength measurable in commercial performance
  • A distinctive, memorable identity that holds up across global markets and compliance requirements
  • Internal teams execute across all touchpoints independently within the brand system
Strategic Impact
The challenge was not making a medical product look approachable. It was narrowing the gap between clinical function, product novelty, and emotional resonance — holding all three with equal credibility, at global scale, without one cancelling out the others.

Case Study 03
Complete Nutrition · Consumer & Clinical

AdVital

Brand Transformation from 2023 · Hospital Strength Launched March 2025

"The formula hasn't changed since 2022. What changed was how the world saw it."

Context

AdVital is a complete nutrition product competing directly against Sustagen, Ensure, and Optifast — with genuine product advantages already built in. Operating across hospital, aged care, community, pharmacy, online, and wholesale channels — primarily B2C, with bulk formats serving B2B. The brand transformation began in 2023. In March 2025, that work enabled the launch of a Hospital Strength clinical product line — made possible entirely by the brand credibility built over the preceding two years.

The Problem

In a competitive category where trust and familiarity drive purchase, AdVital was invisible — not because the product was lacking, but because the brand had no identity to make it seen:

  • Visual identity was manufacturing-grade — no personality, no resonance, overlooked on shelf against established competitors
  • The brand talked about the product, not the person using it — no emotional connection, no guidance on how to integrate it into daily life
  • No cohesive visual system across channels — hospital, pharmacy, and online all felt disconnected, creating a broken experience at every touchpoint
  • No pathway for future product extensions or category growth
Before

AdVital before — manufacturing-grade identity, product-led messaging, no cross-channel cohesion

What I Built

A full brand transformation — repositioning AdVital from daily supplement to daily life companion, with the user at the centre:

  • Repositioned the product around the user — "Add one scoop" became an act of empowerment, not a clinical obligation
  • Shifted brand voice from product-led to person-led — emphasising how AdVital integrates into real life, for real people across every meal and every moment
  • Redesigned packaging to resonate with daily life rather than clinical callouts — a visual language that belongs in a home, not just a hospital
  • Built a cohesive tone of voice and visual system across all channels — enabling brand consistency without designer dependency
  • Created the brand infrastructure that made Hospital Strength possible — launching into clinical and aged care in March 2025 on the back of an established, trusted brand name
After

AdVital after — "Complete Nutrition in Just One Scoop" campaign, Hospital Strength extension, cohesive cross-channel system

Outcome
  • Primary SKU more than doubled — from 27,000 units (July 2022–May 2024) to 58,000 units (July 2024–May 2026) — comparable timeframes, same formula
  • Hospital Strength launched March 2025 into clinical and aged care channels — entering on the strength of an established brand name, not a new one
  • Brand coherence strengthened across both the FMCG market and FSANZ regulatory environment
  • Product line extended and growing — the system built to support it from the start
